## What we worked on

Jordan came in with the Friday quiz on derivative rules. We did:

1. Five power-rule warm-ups. He got all five with no comment.
2. Two product-rule problems. Got the first cleanly. On the second, he wrote
   the form right but lost the second term mid-line — same skipped-step
   pattern Ms. Patel flagged in September.
3. **Three chain-rule problems.** All three he wrote the outer derivative
   correctly and forgot the inner. He noticed himself on the third one and
   said, "wait, the inside" — but only after writing the wrong answer down.

We talked about what's actually going on. His verbatim quote:
*"I know the rule. I just stop reading the function before I'm done."*

That tracks with what I see. He doesn't have a rule-knowledge gap. He has an
attention gap on composite functions specifically. The rule activation comes
~5 seconds late.

## What we tried

I asked him to underline the inner function before differentiating, every
time, no exceptions. We did six more problems with the underline rule.
He got 5/6 right. The one he missed was a $\sin(\sqrt{x})$ — two layers of
composition — where he caught the outer chain but missed the inner-inner.
We agreed two-layer chains are next week's homework.

## What's working

- Power rule: rock solid.
- Product rule: 90% — slip is loss-of-second-term, not concept gap.
- Quotient rule: getting there, sign errors common.
- Underlining the inner before differentiating helps. **Strongly.**

## What's not yet

- Single-layer chain rule on its own: 50/50, attention-driven misses.
- Two-layer compositions: not internalized yet.
- Word problems where the chain rule is hidden inside a setup (e.g. position
  functions where the input is itself a function of t): hasn't seen these
  cleanly yet, will fall apart.

## Recommended next steps

- Keep the Tue/Thu office hours with Ms. Patel.
- 10 minutes/day of just chain rule, with the underline rule, until it's
  automatic. No mixed problem sets — pure repetition on chain only.
- Re-test in 2 weeks.

He's coachable. He doesn't need a different teacher; he needs reps.
