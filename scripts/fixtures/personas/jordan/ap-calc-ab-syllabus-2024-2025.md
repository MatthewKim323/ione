## Course description

A college-level introduction to differential and integral calculus,
following the College Board AP Calculus AB curriculum framework. Successful
completion prepares students for the AP Calculus AB exam in May.

## Learning objectives (selected, abbreviated from CED)

- **LO 2.1A** Identify the derivative of a function as the limit of a difference quotient.
- **LO 2.1C** Calculate derivatives using the power rule.
- **LO 2.1C** Calculate derivatives using the product and quotient rules.
- **LO 2.1C** Calculate derivatives of composite functions using the **chain rule**.
- **LO 2.1D** Calculate derivatives of implicitly defined functions.
- **LO 2.2A** Use derivatives to model rates of change in motion problems.
- **LO 3.1A** Recognize antiderivatives of basic functions.
- **LO 3.3** Apply the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus to evaluate definite integrals.

## Office hours and intervention

Students whose unit-exam grade drops below a B− are **strongly encouraged**
(and in some cases required, with parent/guardian sign-off) to attend at
least one Tue/Thu office-hours session before the next assessment. Common
problem topics from past years that benefit most from in-person support:

- The **chain rule** (Unit 3) — typically the first time students encounter
  composition explicitly. Most missed topic on the December final.
- Implicit differentiation (Unit 4)
- Optimization word problems (Unit 5)
- Volumes of revolution (Unit 8)

If a student misses 3+ chain-rule problems on the Unit 3 final, I reach out
directly to the family and counselor to set up a recovery plan.
